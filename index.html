Investigation 01

The Weather
Constraint

The green line tracks daily mean temperature at the Bindukuri weather station in Burma — the closest recording station to the USAAF's Katha bombing campaign. Each blue dot marks a day when a US mission struck Katha.

Finding
Blue dots cluster around the peaks of the temperature wave — summer months with the highest temperatures. Military planners clearly preferred warmer conditions, likely for better visibility and aircraft performance.
But Strategy Overrides Weather
Missions also appear during winter months — even when temperatures dropped below 15°C. Strategic necessity could not be grounded by the thermometer. Commanders authorized runs regardless of adverse conditions.
ARIMA Seasonal Analysis
The regularity of this seasonal pattern is exactly what makes it statistically predictable. Using an ARIMA(1,0,1) time-series model, we can forecast future temperatures — and therefore optimal mission windows.

Investigation 03

Predicting
the Skies

Using the ARIMA model (Auto-Regressive Integrated Moving Average), we trained a mathematical forecasting system on the Bindukuri temperature record and asked: how well can we predict the past?

Method: ARIMA(1,0,1)
p=1 (lag from PACF), d=0 (data pre-differenced), q=1 (MA term from ACF). The model was trained on 1943–1944 data and evaluated against the full record.
Result: High Accuracy
The predicted line closely traces the actual temperature curve, correctly capturing the seasonal oscillation between 12°C winter lows and 32°C summer peaks. Mean Squared Error was minimized using our ARMA configuration.
Strategic Implication
This proves that with reliable weather station data, military planners could have used mathematical models to predict optimal operational windows months in advance — a capability that would not formally arrive until decades later.

Conclusions

What the Data
Tells Us

Three investigations. Three answers that reveal how data — even 80-year-old data — can decode the logic of war.

Strategy Overrides Climate
While bombers preferred warmer conditions, the data proves weather was not a hard constraint. Commanders authorized missions across all temperature ranges when strategic necessity demanded it. The war machine rarely stopped for the weather.
The Dynamic Center of Gravity
The geographic focus was volatile. Libya dominated 1940–42, Italy surged through 1943–44 as the Mediterranean front opened, and Burma drew intense CBI theater action. Each shift in mission volume maps directly to a historical turning point.
Mathematical Forecasting Works
Our ARIMA model successfully reconstructed the seasonal temperature pattern of the Burma theater with high accuracy. This validates the use of time-series analysis as a tool for understanding — and potentially anticipating — wartime environmental conditions.
